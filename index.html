Andrew O. McLaughlin, PhD

Andrew O. McLaughlin has worked in public service roles over the span of his career. At 17 years old, he enlisted in the military, completing 7 years of service and a deployment to Iraq with the U.S. Army. This experience continues to inform and lend perspective of Andrew as a public servant, researcher, and educator. Andrew holds a PhD in Media and Communications Studies from the University of Oregon’s School of Journalism and Communication where he studied how communication and media censorship policies have shaped the political economy of nonfiction combat footage.

Since completing his PhD, teaching in higher education for 10 years, and working in Chicago as a Veterans Career Coach in a workforce development agencies Homeless Veterans Reintegration Program, Andrew has worked in strategic public health communications, starting with the State of Wisconsin’s COVID-19 response before most recently working as a Digital Communication Content Strategist (GS-1001-23) in CDC’s Office on Smoking and Health (OSH).

Andrew is running enthusiast, choosing a daily practice over speed and distance. He completed the Great Virtual Race Across Tennessee (GVRAT) 1000l in 101 days in 2020. Currently living in Madison, WI, he is is thankful for his access to the areas Driftless region.
