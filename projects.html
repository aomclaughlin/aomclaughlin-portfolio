CDC Tobacco Website Redesign for the Office on Smoking and Health

The CDC Tobacco website had grown into a comprehensive but content-heavy resource. As Content Strategist, I led modernization efforts to improve clarity, accessibility, and equity framing.

Read More

Problem

  • Navigation reflected years of additions rather than a unified structure.
  • Content length and density made scanning difficult for non-experts.
  • Accessibility standards had evolved since the last redesign.
  • Health equity and disparities content was not highlighted in line with CDC’s modern framing.

Solution

  • Streamlined navigation aligned to audience needs (public, providers, public health).
  • Content audit and consolidation to reduce redundancy and simplify pathways.
  • Visual storytelling and improved readability with whitespace, infographics, and feature stories.
  • Accessibility compliance updates aligning with WCAG 2.1 standards.
  • Equity-first presentation elevating disparities and health equity content.

Impact

  • Reduced page depth for top tasks; clearer entrances for priority audiences.
  • Improved readability scores and scan-ability across high-traffic pages.
  • Clearer framing of disparities content and equity guidance.

User Acceptance Testing for CDC & DHS Web Applications

At CDC and the Wisconsin Department of Health Services, I performed user acceptance testing (UAT) on multiple public health web applications, ensuring data accuracy, usability, and functionality across both public-facing and administrative systems.

Read More

Problem

  • Critical public health data systems required validation before release to ensure accuracy and usability.
  • Complex filterable modules, maps, and metadata systems created high risk for mismatches and errors.
  • Large-scale platforms like the STATE System and MCRC served both external users and internal administrators, requiring seamless performance across roles.

Solution

  • Tested STATE System commercial tobacco surveillance dashboards to ensure data backend aligned with front-end filters and visualizations.
  • Conducted validation of metadata integrity, ensuring correct labeling and accessibility compliance.
  • Verified interactive data visualizations, including maps, rendered correctly and aligned with expected parameters.
  • Performed UAT on the Media Campaign Resource Center (MCRC), testing both the customer catalog search interface and the admin-side order processing system.
  • Worked with SMEs and developers to log, prioritize, and resolve bugs before production release.

Impact

  • Improved reliability of CDC data systems that inform national commercial tobacco surveillance and research.
  • Strengthened user trust in MCRC, the world’s largest anti-commercial tobacco advertising catalog, by ensuring smooth ordering and catalog search experience.
  • Reduced downstream support tickets by resolving issues before launch.
  • Ensured public health practitioners and researchers had accurate, accessible tools to guide data-driven decisions.
