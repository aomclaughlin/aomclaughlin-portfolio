Representation and Exploitation of War and Conflict: Publicly Appropriable Media as Low Hanging Fruit

PhD dissertation · University of Oregon · 2024

Analyzes how platform incentives and legal regimes shape exploitation and circulation of graphic war media online.

Read more

Problem

How do platform incentives, legal regimes, and archival access shape the exploitation and circulation of graphic war media online?

Solution

  • Historical analysis of shock media across formats and decades, with attention to the publicly appropriable archive (PAM).
  • Examined impacts of U.S. policy (e.g., Section 230) on distribution and monetization.

Outcome

  • Framework for evaluating ethical representation of conflict imagery; highlighted alternatives such as Dattalion.

Building an efficient workflow tool to support rapidly emerging COVID-19 guidance

State public health · Workflow & automation

Built a Microsoft Lists workflow to triage, assign, and track rapid guidance changes with notifications and audit trail.

Read more

Problem

Rapid guidance changes risked missed updates, unclear ownership, and delays to publication.

Solution

  • Microsoft Lists workflow with fields for source, audience, priority, and change type.
  • Saved views/filters for owners; notifications for assignments and due dates.
  • History log to support QA and leadership reporting.

Outcome

  • Clear triage and ownership; faster turnaround on content updates.
  • Traceable audit trail for retrospectives and compliance.
