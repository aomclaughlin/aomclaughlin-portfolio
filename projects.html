CDC “Clean Slate” & Tobacco Website Redesign — Office on Smoking and Health

Federal Public Health · Content Architecture & UX

Problem

Inconsistent page patterns and complex information architecture made it harder for audiences to find program content and for teams to maintain it.

Solution

  • Unified component patterns (headers, intros, CTAs, related content) into modular templates.
  • Refined navigation and headings for clarity and accessibility.
  • Facilitated cross‑team reviews and documented standards for reuse.

Outcome

  • Improved content consistency and scannability across OSH pages.
  • Reduced editorial effort for updates and new page builds.

building an efficient workflow tool to support rapidly emerging COVID-19 guidance

State Public Health · Workflow & Automation

Problem

Rapid guidance changes risked missed updates, unclear ownership, and delays to publication.

Solution

  • Microsoft Lists workflow with fields for source, audience, priority, and change type.
  • Saved views/filters for owners; notifications for assignments and due dates.
  • History log to support QA and leadership reporting.

Outcome

  • Clear triage and ownership; faster turnaround on content updates.
  • Traceable audit trail for retrospectives and compliance.

STATE System—UAT for Commercial Tobacco Surveillance

Federal Public Health · Data QA & UAT

Problem

Potential mismatches between backend data and UI filters/labels could mislead users and undermine trust in visualizations.

Solution

  • End‑to‑end test cases across filters, views, exports, and map layers.
  • Defect logging with repro steps; partnered with devs to resolution.
  • Spot checks on units, labels, and source notes.

Outcome

  • Higher confidence in data integrity and UI behavior pre‑launch.
  • Smoother handoff to content and analytics teams.
